Skill Levels

How do you pick a level?

It can be challenging picking a level - especially if you have never played in a tournament. An individual may have an idea what level they are at and some have no clue. What upsets a lot of people is when a team intentionally chooses to play at a level lower than they should be playing at. A rule of thumb is if one player is at a 3.5 level (for example) and the partner is at the 3.0 level the team should play at 3.5.

The following is a guide that I put in place that may help you choose a level. I came up with this and others might see it slightly differently but I believe this could be used to help make a choice.

Level 2.5

For people who have been playing for only a short while that do not feel they fit the Novice category (higher than the novice level). Also for lower intermediate players.

Level 3.0

For middle of the road intermediate players.

Level 3.5

For lower to middle advanced players.

Level 4.0

For higher advanced players.

Kally-Anna

One introduction to pickleball clinic with my husband in the summer of 2024 quickly turned into my favorite sport to play. In November 2024 I started playing a few times and week and haven't stopped! At first, it was so my husband, Mitchell and I could have a sport to play together - and while that's still the case - I now play much more often than him! If I could play every day of the week I would!

I have always loved being involved in sports including volleyball, curling, ultimate frisbee, and disc golf. I still play volleyball twice a week during the season, but nothing has caught on like pickleball! I found that having court skills from volleyball helped me adjust to pickleball quickly. I also love that pickleball can be played both inside and outdoors all year round. I played as much as I could, building my fundamentals with a great crew in St. Norbert. Then in the summer of 2025, my husband and I joined friends in an outdoor league which helped us met even more fun people in the pickleball community.

As someone who works full-time as a Speech Therapist during the week, I'm always looking for games in the evenings and weekends - where ever I am - to stay active and connected! I continue to play in leagues and even some tournaments. If you somehow find me not playing pickleball, I also love time spent with my incredible friends - especially ones with dogs, enjoying all the great restaurants in the city, shopping at local markets, and spending time with family anywhere where I can put a paddle board in the lake.

Debbie & Garry

Our pickleball history started in the fall of 2014 in Mesa Arizona. For the next six years we were lucky to winter in Mesa where we were fortunate to have weekly lessons. We were blessed to have amazing, positive teachers who encouraged us to keep challenging ourselves. Even after a few weeks as beginners when we were feeling less than confident of our skills, they encouraged us to participate in the Saturday morning scrambles, which were mini tournaments complete with medals each week. They instilled confidence every step of the way of our pickleball journey.

Our super organized club at Venture Out RV park hosted many large tournaments where we were encouraged to take training in being line judges and take referee training. Our last two winters there we were invited to teach beginners - once again pushing us beyond our comfort zone at the start, but knowing that we were ready to take that step.

Of course after experiencing such welcoming people within the Arizona pickleball circle, we couldn't wait to engage pickleball in Winnipeg. Once life got back to ‚Äúnormal‚Äù after the Covid pause, we've enjoyed play at Dakota Community Club in St. Vital and Fort Garry Mennonite Brethren on Pembina Highway.

Pickleball is ideal for keeping the body active and we continue to learn new ways to keep improving our skills by taking classes and watching videos. Pickleball has tons of benefits: great exercise, helps keep your memory sharp and a great way to meet new like-minded folks.

No matter where we travel we can almost always find a court and meet new pickleball friends.
